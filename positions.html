politics

My position on politics is complex and I don't think I'm at a point where I can say I confidently feel one way or another. On one hand, it is evidently a tool of the bourgeoisie to further their class interest. That is immutably the case, as the entire congressional body of "representatives" consists of rich business people, lobbyists of large corporate industries, bourgeois actors, or opportunists professing to progressivism or socialism, whilst only serving capitalist interests in their practices.
At the same time, there really is not much the proletariat presently has a grasp on to further its interests. To dismiss the electoral process entirely is reasonable, but I struggle to see what exactly can be done beyond it. Almost all leftist "organization" we see in the western world has been either outright opportunist, co-opted by bourgeois actors, or forcefully shut down by the bourgeois state.
I find the trap most leftists fall into when discussing present events is they persistently relate them into future "communist" or "socialist" conditions. Every critique those leftists make of any present capitalist event or condition is always linked to their utopian future socialism, and it always seems so foolish to me. How can we possibly have any conception of conditions expanding so far into the future? Linking capitalist conditions to communist ones is misunderstanding the nature of communism and how it realizes in society. We can never truly know if the steps we take now are closer to communism, to fascism, or will just reproduce capital once again. there's simply too many variables for us to accurately plan ahead.

I believe that there is no optimal path forward from this point.
